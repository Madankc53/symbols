National Museum of Nepal

National Museum — राष्ट्रिय संग्रहालय

The National Museum of Nepal, in Chhauni on the edge of Kathmandu, is the country's oldest and largest museum. Established in 1928 as an arsenal displaying weapons and war trophies, it has grown into the principal institution preserving Nepal's art, history and natural heritage.

Tucked into the old military quarter of Chhauni, on a low ridge overlooking the Kathmandu Valley, the National Museum began life not as a place of art appreciation but as a storehouse of weapons — a legacy still visible in its oldest galleries today, even as the institution has grown to hold centuries of Nepali sculpture, painting and natural history.

From Arsenal to National Museum

The museum's origins trace back to 1928, when it opened inside a former army barracks complex known as Chhauni Silkhana, displaying weapons, armour and war trophies collected from Nepal's military campaigns, including the Anglo-Nepalese War. This early collection reflected the martial character of the site itself, which had long served administrative and military functions under the Rana regime.

Over subsequent decades, the museum's scope broadened well beyond weaponry. Additional buildings were incorporated to house sculpture, paintings, coins and archaeological finds, transforming what began as a specialised arms display into a general historical and cultural museum representing Nepal's artistic and political history.

The complex today comprises several buildings, each with its own focus, allowing the museum to present a wide range of material — from centuries-old stone carvings to taxidermied wildlife specimens — without crowding it all into a single gallery space.

Collections and Highlights

Among its most notable holdings, the museum houses an extensive weapons collection documenting Nepal's military history, along with stone and metal sculptures depicting Hindu and Buddhist deities that trace the evolution of Nepali religious art across centuries. Coin collections offer a window into the economic history of the Kathmandu Valley's historic kingdoms, predating the unified Nepalese Rupee.

A separate Buddhist Art Gallery within the complex focuses specifically on thangka paintings, bronze statues and ritual objects, while the adjoining Natural History Museum, though administered separately, shares the same historic grounds and displays specimens of Nepal's flora and fauna.

✦ Did you know?

  • The museum complex sits on the former grounds of a military barracks, and some of its buildings still carry traces of that original use.
  • Chhauni takes its name from a term associated with military garrisons under the Rana administration.
  • The museum's weapons gallery includes firearms and swords used during the Anglo-Nepalese War of the early 19th century.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the national museum of Nepal?

The National Museum of Nepal, located in Chhauni, Kathmandu, is the country's oldest and largest museum, housing collections of weapons, art, coins and historical artefacts spanning Nepal's history.

When was the National Museum of Nepal established?

The museum traces its origins to 1928, when it opened as an arsenal museum displaying weapons and war trophies, before later expanding into a general historical and cultural museum.

Where is the National Museum located?

The National Museum of Nepal is located in Chhauni, on the western edge of Kathmandu, within a historic building complex that once served military purposes.

What can visitors see at the National Museum?

Visitors can see historical weapons and armour, sculptures and artwork spanning Nepal's Buddhist and Hindu traditions, coins, and separate buildings dedicated to natural history and to the Buddhist art collection.

Is the National Museum different from other museums in Kathmandu?

Yes. The National Museum in Chhauni is distinct from other Kathmandu Valley museums such as the Patan Museum and Hanuman Dhoka Palace Museum, and is considered the principal historical museum representing Nepal at a national level.
