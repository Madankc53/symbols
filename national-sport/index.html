National Sport of Nepal

Volleyball — भलिबल

Volleyball is the official national sport of Nepal, declared by the Government of Nepal in 2017. Played in villages, army barracks and city courts alike, it earned its status through sheer grassroots reach, standing today as the most widely played competitive sport across the country.

Drive through almost any Nepali town or village and you'll spot a volleyball net strung between two poles, often improvised from bamboo, with a game underway regardless of the hour. That everyday ubiquity is exactly why, in 2017, the government made official what was already true in practice: volleyball is Nepal's national sport.

How Volleyball Became Official

The Government of Nepal formally declared volleyball the national sport in 2017, a decision made through the Ministry of Youth and Sports in recognition of the game's exceptional grassroots reach. Unlike sports that thrive mainly in cities, volleyball had already spread into rural hill villages, army and police barracks, and school playgrounds across every province, making it a genuinely nationwide pastime rather than an urban or elite pursuit.

The designation gave volleyball a formal status alongside Nepal's other national symbols, and it has since been used to justify greater government investment in courts, coaching and domestic tournaments meant to build competitive strength at home and internationally.

Source: Ministry of Youth and Sports, Government of Nepal

Official Sport vs. Traditional Game

Two different traditions

Since 2017
Volleyball

Nepal's officially declared national sport, governed by the Volleyball Association of Nepal and played competitively nationwide.

Traditional Game
Dandi Biyo

A traditional stick-and-peg game long associated with rural Nepal, popularly called a national game though never given official status.

Before volleyball's formal declaration, Dandi Biyo — a game played with two sticks, a smaller peg struck and launched with a longer stick — was often informally referred to as Nepal's national game. It remains a beloved village pastime and a marker of rural Nepali childhood, even though it was never granted the legal national-sport status that volleyball now holds.

Volleyball's Reach in Nepal

Volleyball's rise owes much to its low barrier to entry: a net, a ball and a patch of flat ground are enough to start a game, making it accessible in places where football pitches or cricket grounds are harder to maintain. The Nepal Army and Nepal Police have historically fielded some of the country's strongest teams, and inter-district and national tournaments draw enthusiastic local followings.

Nepal's national volleyball teams, both men's and women's, compete in South Asian regional tournaments, and the sport continues to be a fixture of school sports days and community festivals across the country.

✦ Did you know?

  • Volleyball courts in rural Nepal are often improvised, using bamboo poles and hand-strung nets rather than official equipment.
  • The Nepal Army and Nepal Police have long been considered among the country's strongest domestic volleyball teams.
  • Dandi Biyo, the traditional game once informally called a national pastime, is still played in many villages during festival seasons.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the national sport of Nepal?

Volleyball is the official national sport of Nepal, formally declared by the Government of Nepal's Ministry of Youth and Sports in 2017.

When did volleyball become Nepal's national sport?

The Government of Nepal officially designated volleyball as the national sport in 2017, recognising its widespread popularity from villages to cities across the country.

Which body governs volleyball in Nepal?

The Volleyball Association of Nepal is the national governing body responsible for organising domestic competitions and overseeing Nepal's national volleyball teams.

Was there a traditional national sport before volleyball?

Before volleyball's official designation, Dandi Biyo, a traditional stick-and-peg game, was often popularly regarded as Nepal's traditional national game, though it never held official government status.

Why was volleyball chosen as Nepal's national sport?

Volleyball was chosen because of its exceptional grassroots popularity across Nepal's hills, plains and urban centres, requiring minimal equipment and being played informally in villages, schools and army barracks nationwide.
